KINTSUGI

Picturebook zine

  • 32 pages (24 + 8 page insert)
  • Zine: 29 x 19,7 cm
  • 3 Color Risoprint
  • Red + Blue + Metallic Gold
  • Original Artworks: 34,4 x 25,4 cm
  • Watercolor + Colored Pencil + Wax Crayon
  • January 2020

Kintsugi is a risograph picturebook printed in zine format, and it is an expansion on the popular english expression “[like a] bull in a china shop”. Unlike its portuguese equivalent (“elephant in a china shop”), the usage of the bull in the english variant evokes a crime of anger, agression and passion.
As such, this interpretation delves into the reasons why a bull would destroy a china shop: it explores cycles of abuse, cruelty, wrath and destruction. Beyond its first reading lies a powerful metaphor for how we deal with our failed relationships.
Two different techniques (and colors) were chosen to better represent this clash of energies: blue watercolor for the serene, delicate porcelaine, and red crayons for the restless, tempestuous bull.
