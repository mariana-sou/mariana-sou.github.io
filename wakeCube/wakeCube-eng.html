Wake,
By The Antlers

On Letting People In

  • Origami Cube
  • Folded: 4cm x 4cm x 4cm
  • Unfolded: 20cm x 20cm
  • Digital Illustration + Analog texture frottage
  • 2018

This project is an interpretation of the song “Wake”, taking into account the events of “Hospice”, a dream pop / post-rock conceptual album by The Antlers, presenting the story of an emotionally abuse relationship between the narrator, a hospice worker, and a terminally-ill patient.
The cube format symbolizes the room in which the narrator has isolated himself, and so the act of unfolding the origami to reveal the inner illustrations becomes a representation of the narrator’s friends’ effort to comprehend what happened to him when he’s finally able to let people in.
Each illustration follows a non-linear, modular structure, meant to depict the narrator’s attempts to piece together scattered memories. It takes full advantage of the folds and different unfolding sequences, organizing the order and rhytmn in which different moments of the narrative are revealed. Therefore, each user’s own unfolding approach offers up new possibilities, the act of deconstruction being crucial for its interpretation.
